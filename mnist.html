DEEP DIVE

What the model learned

📦

MNIST DATASET

Modified National Institute of Standards and Technology — compiled in 1998. Still one of the most widely used benchmarks in deep learning history.

🔗

FULLY CONNECTED

Every input pixel is connected to every hidden neuron. At 784 inputs × 128 hidden units, that's over 100,000 weights trained on digit patterns.

🎲

SOFTMAX

The output layer converts raw scores (logits) into a probability distribution. All 10 probabilities sum to 1.0 — the model is always making a bet.

📉

SPARSE CROSS-ENTROPY

Loss = −log(p_correct). Wrong confident predictions get penalised heavily — nudging all weights to improve with each mini-batch.

🧠

WHAT NEURONS LEARN

Hidden neurons learn stroke orientations, curve directions, and loop detectors — visualised, they look like digit fragments.

⚡

WHY IT'S FAST

28×28 is tiny. ~100K multiplications per image, handled entirely in your GPU/CPU via TensorFlow.js — no round-trip to any server.
